Mochi Brownies

Description

Mochi brownies are a chocolatey and rich dessert, with a twist on a regular brownie with a soft, yet dense and chewy inside, and a crispy outside. You can add extra toppings like chocolate chips, nuts, or powdered sugar dusted on top.

Note: These can last at room temp in a sealed container for 3-4 days, or cooled completely then frozen for 1-2 months, and thawed at room temperature before eating.

Ingredients

  • 90g (6 tbsp) unsalted butter
  • 4g (2 tsp) instant coffee powder
  • 130g (1 cup) glutinous rice flour
  • 150g (3/4 cup) granulated sugar
  • 40g (1/2 cup) cocoa powder
  • 7g (1 1/2 tsp) baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 360ml (1 1/2 cup) whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 100g dark chocolate (roughly chopped)
  • flakey salt (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C) and line an 8-inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. Melt the butter in a small saucepan, around 3 minutes, then heat for another 3-5 minutes until fragrant and there are brown specks on the bottom of the pan.
  3. Remove from heat and add coffee powder to the saucepan. Set aside to cool for 15 minutes until room temperature.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour, cocoa powder, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Whisk till combined.
  5. Next, add milk, the cooled brown butter and egg. Whisk till smooth.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and scatter on top the chopped chocolate.
  7. Bake for 45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out slightly gooey.
  8. Cool the pan on a wire rack completely, then remove the brownies from the pan, and sprinkle with flakey salt. Cut into 12 squares and enjoy~

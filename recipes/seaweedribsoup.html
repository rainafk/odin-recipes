Dried Seaweed and Rib Soup

Description

Seaweed and Rib soup is a staple Chinese pork bone soup. Using ribs gives less fat and produces a lighter flavour, and dried seaweed gives umami. This can be served as a side to contrast with hot sesame noodles, buns or steamed rice.

Note: Recommend using a pressure cooker so it can simmer on a low heat with less time needed for a clean and light soup taste.

Ingredients

  • 80g dried seaweed / kelp (hydrated)
  • 4L water
  • 500g pork ribs (chopped into bite-sized pieces)
  • green onion (knotted)
  • 1 thumb ginger (smashed)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • cilantro (garnish)

Steps

  1. Heat a pot of water until boiling, then put the ribs and green onion in. Cook for 2-3 minutes, transfer out, wash and rinse.
  2. Cut the dried seaweed into small pieces.
  3. Put the water, ginger, dried seaweed and ribs into a high-pressure cooker. Cook using the soup option, usually about 30 minutes.
  4. When finished, add salt then cover for 5-10 minutes before serving.
